<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading">List of <i>Billboard</i> number-one singles</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<div id="contentSub2"></div>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For the current year's number one hits, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_Billboard_Hot_100_number_ones_of_2021" title="List of Billboard Hot 100 number ones of 2021">List of Billboard Hot 100 number ones of 2021</a>.</div>
<p>This is a list of songs that have peaked at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and the magazine's national singles charts that preceded it. Introduced in 1958, the Hot 100 is the pre-eminent <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a> chart in the <a href="/wiki/Music_of_the_United_States" title="Music of the United States">United States</a>, currently monitoring the most popular singles in terms of popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_play" title="Radio play">radio play</a>,  single purchases and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Online_streaming" title="Online streaming">online streaming</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preface">Preface</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Billboard_number-one_singles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preface">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following year-by-year, week-by-week listings are based on statistics accrued by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine before and after the inception of its Hot 100 popularity chart in August 1958.
</p><p>All data like to play within the United States. Later charts also include digital single sales, online streaming, and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> hits.
</p>
